Objectives & mission

Our objectives are :

1. To reduce unemployment among vulnerable groups by providing practical, inclusive, and hands on skills that lead directly to income.
2. To enhance the entrepreneurial capacities of community members, especially those in the informal sector.
3. To ensure inclusive economic empowerment for people with disabilities, recognizing their rights and potential.
4. To ignite entrepreneurship among youth, women, and PWDs by turning survival skills into thriving businesses.
5. To empower women and persons with disabilities with value-added skills such as bakery, crafts, and modern farming methods.
6. To promote true independence by offering skills instead of handouts.
7. To rebuild confidence through business mentorship and practical product skills training.
8. To connect beneficiaries with markets by establishing direct and long-term linkages for products and services through trade shows, entrepreneur benchmarking, and field visits.

Mission

Our mission is to empower youth, women, and people with disabilities in the informal sector by providing them with the necessary tools, knowledge, and resources to create sustainable businesses. The organization aims to reduce unemployment and enhance the entrepreneurial capacities of marginalized communities.

Our vision is to create a model, inclusive and empowered society in Uganda.
We are currently running the Magic Hands Initiative which empowers marginalized rural populations, particularly youth, women, and persons with disabilities (PWDs), by strengthening their entrepreneurial and business development skills through financial literacy, entrepreneurship training, digital literacy, business mentorship, and value-added skills training.

Problems addressed by the Magic Hands in our community

The project is addressing several deep-rooted community problems, especially those affecting the marginalized rural populations in Mukono District, and these include the following:
1. Unemployment Among Women, Youth, and Persons with Disabilities (PWDs)
2. Lack of Marketable and Business Skills
3. Social Exclusion and Stigmatization of PWDs
4. Poverty and Dependency on Handouts
5. Limited Financial Literacy
6. Low Confidence and Lack of Role Models
7. Disconnected Local Markets
